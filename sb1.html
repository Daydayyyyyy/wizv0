Understanding Phishing

What is It ?

Phishing is a deceptive practice that cybercriminals use to trick individuals into revealing sensitive information such as passwords and credit card numbers. It’s like fishing, but instead of trying to catch fish, these fraudsters are trying to catch your personal details. Scary, right?

Understanding Phishing

  • 1 Definition of Phishing: Understanding what phishing is and how it works is the first step towards preventing it.
  • 2 Types of Phishing Attacks: There are various types of phishing attacks, each with unique characteristics and methods.
  • 3 Potential Risks and Impacts: Phishing attacks can lead to severe consequences, including financial loss and data breaches.

Types of Phishing Attacks

  • 1 Email Phishing: This is the most common type of phishing, where attackers send fraudulent emails that appear to be from reputable sources.
  • 2 Spear Phishing: This is a targeted form of phishing where the attacker has studied their victim and customized their attack.
  • 3 Whaling: This type of attack targets high-profile individuals like CEOs and CFOs.
  • 4 Smishing and Vishing: These are phishing attacks that are conducted through SMS and voice calls respectively.
Imagine you receive an email that appears to be from your bank, asking you to update your account details. You click on the link in the email and are taken to a website that looks just like your bank’s. You enter your account details, only to find out later that you’ve been phished and your account has been drained.
Now, imagine a similar scenario, but this time the email is specifically tailored to you, with your name and other personal details. This is spear phishing.
In a whaling attack, the CEO of your company might receive an email that appears to be from a trusted source, asking for sensitive company information.
Smishing and vishing attacks would involve receiving a text message or a voice call, respectively, attempting to trick you into giving away personal information.
